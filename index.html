BCC develops when there’s an uncontrolled growth of the skin’s basal cells, which line the deepest layer of the epidermis — the skin’s outermost layer. It almost never spreads to other parts of the body and it’s rarely fatal, although it can invade surrounding tissue and be disfiguring if it’s not treated. Squamous cell carcinoma of the skin is a common form of skin cancer that develops in the squamous cells that make up the middle and outer layers of the skin. Squamous cell carcinoma of the skin is usually not life-threatening, though it can be aggressive.
